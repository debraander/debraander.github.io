Tails

Most artificial flies just don't look right without a tail; after all, the majority of the naturals you are imitating have a tail of some type.

  • Hackle-Fiber
  • Hair
  • Marabou
  • Biot

Tails of this type are now the most common. Hackle-fiber tails are made from rooster or hen hackle and resemble a natural insect tail.

Hair tails are used on many dry-fly patterns. Beginning flytiers should practice making hair tails using calf or elk hair; deer hair tends to flare around the hook shank. You will need a hair stacker for aligning the tips.

Marabou tails seem to breathe and pulsate under water, giving almost any pattern a lifelike appearance.

Biot tails are generally shorter than other types of tails and are tied in with a different technique. Biots can also be used as a substitute for hackle-fiber tail on many nymphs and wet flies.
