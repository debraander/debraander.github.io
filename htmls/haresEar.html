Gold Ribbed Hare's Ear

This scruffy nymph has proven consistently attractive to trout and anglers everywhere.

  • Details
  • Materials
  • Tutorial

Also known as The Hare's Ear, this fly gets its unusual name from the soft, absorbant fibers of a hare's mask which is used for the body and tail of the fly.

With this fly, you will have an opportunity to practice dubbing; you will also rib with oval gold tinsel and create a wing case. These techniques are valuable and are used frequently.

The Gold Ribbed Hare's Ear can be swum from deep to shallow in streams or lakes, or fished dead drift in streams. While it is generally weighted with a few turns of lead under the thorax, unweighted versions are generally best for fishing near the surface.

  • Hook: Mustad 3906; 8-16
  • Thread: Tan or Brown 6/0
  • Wingcase: Brown Mottled Turkey-Quill Section
  • Tail: Hare's Mask Guard Hairs or Brown Hackle Fibers
  • Rib: Flat or Oval Gold Tinsel or Copper Wire
  • Abdomen: Hare's Mask Dubbing
  • Thorax: Hare's Mask Dubbing
