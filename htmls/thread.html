Thread

Fly-tying thread secures the materials onto the hook while adding as little bulk as possible. When choosing a fly-tying thread, consider diameter, material, and strength.

Until recently, fly-tying thread was made of silk, and many flytiers still feel it is superior, but the majority of flytiers have turned to synthetic threads. Synthetics allow you to use finer diameter threads without sacrificing strength. They come in a wider choice of colors and are available prewaxed, which means they hold materials better than silk does.

Recent innovations in thread include new materials, such as Kevlar and Spectra, which are virtually unbreakable.
