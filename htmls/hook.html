Hooks

The hook is the foundation of any fly pattern. In most cases, the novice flytier need only be concerned with two hook styles: the dry-fly and wet-fly.

Hooks for dry flies are made with light-gauge wire to help them float on the surface. Wet-fly hooks, which include streamer and nymph hooks, are generally heavier to help them sink quickly.

The size, length, wire and shape of a hook all determine how the finished pattern will look and behave on or in the water.
