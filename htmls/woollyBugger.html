Woolly Bugger

The woolly bugger is categorized as a wet fly or streamer. It is the most recognizable, and likely the most commonly fished streamer fly ever tied.

  • Details
  • Materials
  • Tutorial
  • Where to Fish

The wooly bugger attracts fish in fast or slow water, rivers, ponds, and lakes, in dirty water or spring creeks. It imitates crayfish, minnows, sculpin, leeches, and many other natural foods trout love.

This streamer fly pattern is a classic that you simply can't be without.

  • Hook: Allen S402 #6
  • Thread: UTC Ultrathread 70 Denier, Black
  • Tail: Black Marabou
  • Ribbing: UTC Ultrawire, Copper-Brown, BR
  • Body: Black/Red Chenille
  • Hackle: Black Schlappen Hackle

You can catch trout all day long at Rattlesnake Lake with a nicely tied Woolly Bugger. It's also just a short drive from Seattle!
